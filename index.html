📌 Assumptions & Financial Logic

1. Revenue & Variable COS
Variable Items: Fare, Ancillary, and Scheduled Flight revenue scale directly with Pax. COS items like Fuel, User Charges, and Station fees are treated as Variable Costs scaling with flown capacity. Fuel is adjusted by the input index (Base 200). Distribution is 3% of Net Revenue.
2. Semi-Fixed & Fixed Costs
Semi-Fixed: Maintenance & Overhaul scales with flown capacity assuming existing fleet utilization. Ramp operational costs are also semi-fixed (80% variable). Fixed Costs: Aircraft Rental, Salaries (Admin), Insurance, and G&A are treated as fixed monthly obligations regardless of capacity changes.
3. Cash Flow Dynamics
Cash inflow is derived from the monthly collection rate applied to Net Revenue. Debenture inflow of 1,500M THB is added specifically in June. Cash outflows assume 100% of P&L expenses (except D&A) are paid within the month to show immediate liquidity impact.
4. Liquidity Rollover Logic
Ending cash is strictly cumulative. April starts with BGN cash (1,200M). May beginning cash equals April ending cash. June beginning cash equals May ending cash. This ensures the "Strategic Matrix" correctly reflects the actual bank balance at each month-end.
